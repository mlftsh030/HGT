Overview

Visit Langa, Cape Town's oldest black township, on a walking tour led by local guides from the community itself. You'll see the historic worker hostels and family homes, meet local entrepreneurs and artists, and experience a side of Cape Town most visitors never see.

Depending on the day, your visit may include traditional gumboot dancing, live music, or a stop for local cuisine — all led by people who live and work in Langa, supporting the local economy directly. See Rules & Requirements below before you book.

Rules & Requirements

Langa is a living community, not a tourist attraction — always ask before photographing residents or their homes.
This tour is led exclusively by guides based in Langa, ensuring your visit directly supports the local community.
The tour covers uneven ground on foot — comfortable, closed-toe shoes are recommended.
Groups are kept small (max 12) for a more personal, respectful experience.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sunblock and a hat
  • Cash for local food, crafts or entrepreneurs
  • An open, respectful attitude

Tour Plan

We meet at the Guga S’thebe Cultural Centre, a hub for local arts and culture in Langa.
See the worker hostels built in the 1920s, and hear about the history of Langa as Cape Town's oldest township.
Visit local businesses and community art projects, meeting the people behind them.
Depending on availability, enjoy traditional gumboot dancing or live local music to close out the visit.
