Overview

Skip the tourist strip and get the real Cape Town night out — open-air shisanyama, premium lounges, and the biggest Amapiano and Afro-house sets in the city, all in Khayelitsha where the culture actually lives. This package bundles secure transport, entry and a proper feast into one straight price, so all you have to do is show up ready to dance.

The Kasi Lifestyle Package

R1,500 / person, all-in

Everything you need for one unforgettable Kasi night out.

  • Secure return transport from your accommodation
  • VIP / general access entry to your chosen venue
  • Premium shisanyama platter — beef, pork, chicken wings & sausage with pap, chakalaka & steamed bread
  • A bottle of premium spirit or a bucket of ciders/beers
  • Dedicated security & a secure, guided environment all night

Venues on This Package

Pick a venue when you book, or let us build a route across more than one in a single night.

Rands Cape Town
4.5 · Khayelitsha 6 Makhabeni Road, Khaya Bazzar

An iconic open-air lifestyle landmark and meat emporium, famous for "Rands Sundays" and massive summer sessions with top SA DJs. Unspent alcohol must stay on-site — it isn't taken back to your vehicle.

Kwa Ace
4.5 · Khayelitsha 158 Phakamani Road

A trendy, recently relaunched multi-level lounge known as "The Castle" — rooftop seating, General/VIP/VVIP sections, and a dedicated deep-house upper floor. Has hosted artists like Black Coffee.

The Milk Restaurant & Bar
4.2 · Khayelitsha Cnr Makabeni & Zantsi St

A first-class three-storey lifestyle venue — boutique ground-floor lounge, first-floor restaurant, and a rooftop bar known for a high-spending clientele and premium champagne.

Teez Lounge
4.6 · Gugulethu & Langa NY 115, Gugulethu

Carrying on the historic Mzoli's kasi chill-spot vibe — Chesterfield couches, quality sound, and a butcher onsite for fresh braai meat.

Saida's Premium Lounge
4.6 · Khayelitsha A385B Maphongwane, Site C

A casual yet trendy community spot, popular for "Premium Shandis" events and local spinning sessions.

Rules & Requirements

A physical South African ID or original passport is mandatory for entry at every venue on this package, used to verify age. Most events are 18+, though some are 21+ or 23+.
Many venues charge a corkage fee (roughly R50–R150) if you bring your own cooler box, usually with a cut-off time such as 2 PM. Rands specifically requires unspent/unopened alcohol to stay on-site rather than returning to your car.
Popular spots like Rands and Teez Lounge reach capacity fast on weekends. We recommend arriving before 2 PM for daytime sessions to secure a table, and booking evening slots in advance.
Weapons and illegal substances are strictly prohibited. Right of admission is reserved and enforced by professional security teams at every venue.
